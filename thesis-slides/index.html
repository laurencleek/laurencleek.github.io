Puzzle

How can highly independent central banks remain legitimate while talking more—and about more—than ever before?

Introduction

  • Parallel rise of independence and communication.
  • Paradox: technocrats seek public support beyond narrow mandates.
  • Thesis view: independence is dynamic; talk both shapes & reflects threats.

Chapter 1
The Rise of Independence & Communication

Research Question

Why did legal independence and proactive communication expand together after 1990?

Methods

  • Global panel (119 CBs, 1997‑2024) of statutes & speeches.
  • Sequence analysis of reforms & content trends.

Findings

  • Independence ≠ one‑off grant; requires continuous responsiveness.
  • Societal & financial pressures, not just inflation history, drive reforms.

Chapter 2
Independence → Monetary‑Policy Talk

Research Question

Does greater independence change what central banks say about monetary policy?

Methods

  • Gemini‑based sentence tagging (monetary vs. financial).
  • Staggered DiD on 100 CBs (1997‑2023) + IV.

Findings

  • ↑ Independence ⇒ ↓ price‑stability talk; ↑ financial‑stability talk.
  • Effect strongest in advanced democracies; robust to crises controls.

Chapter 3
Who Sets the Eurosystem Agenda?

Research Question

Do NCBs or the ECB introduce new policy topics first?

Methods

  • BERTopic on 4 659 speeches (1997‑2024).
  • First‑mover graphs & Markov chains.

Findings

  • NCBs lead on climate & supervision; ECB follows.
  • Coalition of NCBs ↑ probability topic diffuses to ECB.

Chapter 4
Textual Indices of Policy Linkages

Research Question

Can LLMs detect shifts toward dominance or coordination across policy domains?

Methods

  • GPT‑3.5 multi‑stage prompting → 2 M sentences → 6 regimes.
  • Validation vs. human gold set.

Findings

  • Mon‑fiscal coordination rises in crises; fiscal dominance rare in EMU.
  • Financial dominance clusters around market‑stress events.

Chapter 5
Conclusion – More Than Talk?

Research Question

How does communication safeguard legitimacy of autonomous central banks?

Methods

Synthesis of empirical chapters + current‑affairs triangulation.

Findings

  • Talk is an adaptive shield, not window‑dressing.
  • Transparency used strategically against evolving threats.
